ModelPredictiveControl.init_estimstoch â€” Function
init_estimstoch(i_ym, nint_ym::Vector{Int})

Calc stochastic model matrices from output integrators specifications for state estimation.

For closed-loop state estimators. nint_ym is a vector providing how many integrator should be added for each measured output $\mathbf{y^m}$. The argument generates the Asm and Csm matrices:

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{x_s}(k+1) &= \mathbf{A_s^m x_s}(k) + \mathbf{B_s^m e}(k) \\ \mathbf{y_s^m}(k) &= \mathbf{C_s^m x_s}(k) \end{aligned}\]

where $\mathbf{e}(k)$ is a conceptual and unknown zero mean white noise. $\mathbf{B_s^m}$ is not used for closed-loop state estimators thus ignored.

ModelPredictiveControl.init_internalmodel â€” Function
init_internalmodel(As, Bs, Cs, Ds)

Calc stochastic model update matrices AÌ‚s and BÌ‚s for InternalModel estimator.

As, Bs, Cs and Ds are the stochastic model matrices :

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{x_s}(k+1) &= \mathbf{A_s x_s}(k) + \mathbf{B_s e}(k) \\ \mathbf{y_s}(k) &= \mathbf{C_s x_s}(k) + \mathbf{D_s e}(k) \end{aligned}\]

where $\mathbf{e}(k)$ is conceptual and unknown zero mean white noise. Its optimal estimation is $\mathbf{eÌ‚=0}$, the expected value. Thus, the AÌ‚s and BÌ‚s matrices that optimally update the stochastic estimate $\mathbf{xÌ‚_s}$ are:

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{xÌ‚_s}(k+1) &= \mathbf{(A_s - B_s D_s^{-1} C_s) xÌ‚_s}(k) + \mathbf{(B_s D_s^{-1}) yÌ‚_s}(k) \\ &= \mathbf{AÌ‚_s xÌ‚_s}(k) + \mathbf{BÌ‚_s yÌ‚_s}(k) \end{aligned}\]

with current stochastic outputs estimation $\mathbf{yÌ‚_s}(k)$, composed of the measured $\mathbf{yÌ‚_s^m}(k) = \mathbf{y^m}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚_d^m}(k)$ and unmeasured $\mathbf{yÌ‚_s^u = 0}$ outputs. See [1].

update_estimate!(estim::KalmanFilter, u, ym, d)

Update KalmanFilter state estim.xÌ‚ and estimation error covariance estim.PÌ‚.

It implements the time-varying Kalman Filter in its predictor (observer) form :

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{M}(k) &= \mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)\mathbf{CÌ‚^m}' [\mathbf{CÌ‚^m PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)\mathbf{CÌ‚^m + RÌ‚}]^{-1} \\ \mathbf{K}(k) &= \mathbf{AÌ‚ M(k)} \\ \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) &= \mathbf{CÌ‚^m xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) + \mathbf{DÌ‚_d^m d}(k) \\ \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \mathbf{AÌ‚ xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) + \mathbf{BÌ‚_u u}(k) + \mathbf{BÌ‚_d d}(k) + \mathbf{K}(k)[\mathbf{y^m}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k)] \\ \mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \mathbf{AÌ‚}[\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) - \mathbf{M}(k)\mathbf{CÌ‚^m PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)]\mathbf{AÌ‚}' + \mathbf{QÌ‚} \end{aligned}\]

based on the process model described in SteadyKalmanFilter. The notation $\mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)$ refers to the state for the current time $k$ estimated at the last control period $k-1$. See [2] for details.

update_estimate!(estim::UnscentedKalmanFilter, u, ym, d)

Update UnscentedKalmanFilter state estim.xÌ‚ and covariance estimate estim.PÌ‚.

It implements the unscented Kalman Filter in its predictor (observer) form, based on the generalized unscented transform[3]. See init_ukf for the definition of the constants $\mathbf{mÌ‚, SÌ‚}$ and $Î³$.

Denoting $\mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)$ as the state for the current time $k$ estimated at the last period $k-1$, $\mathbf{0}$, a null vector, $n_Ïƒ = 2 n_\mathbf{xÌ‚} + 1$, the number of sigma points, and $\mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^j(k)$, the vector at the $j$th column of $\mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)$, the estimator updates the states with:

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) &= \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) & \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) & \cdots & \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) \end{matrix}\bigg] + \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{0} & Î³ \sqrt{\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)} & -Î³ \sqrt{\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)} \end{matrix}\bigg] \\ \mathbf{YÌ‚^m}(k) &= \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{hÌ‚^m}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{1}(k) \Big) & \mathbf{hÌ‚^m}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{2}(k) \Big) & \cdots & \mathbf{hÌ‚^m}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{n_Ïƒ}(k) \Big) \end{matrix}\bigg] \\ \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) &= \mathbf{YÌ‚^m}(k) \mathbf{mÌ‚} \\ \mathbf{XÌ„}_{k-1}(k) &= \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{1}(k) - \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) & \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{2}(k) - \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) & \cdots & \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k-1}^{n_Ïƒ}(k) - \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) \end{bmatrix} \\ \mathbf{YÌ„^m}(k) &= \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{YÌ‚^m}^{1}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) & \mathbf{YÌ‚^m}^{2}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) & \cdots & \mathbf{YÌ‚^m}^{n_Ïƒ}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) \end{bmatrix} \\ \mathbf{M}(k) &= \mathbf{YÌ„^m}(k) \mathbf{SÌ‚} \mathbf{YÌ„^m}'(k) + \mathbf{RÌ‚} \\ \mathbf{K}(k) &= \mathbf{XÌ„}_{k-1}(k) \mathbf{SÌ‚} \mathbf{YÌ„^m}'(k) \mathbf{M}(k)^{-1} \\ \mathbf{xÌ‚}_k(k) &= \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) + \mathbf{K}(k) \big[ \mathbf{y^m}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) \big] \\ \mathbf{PÌ‚}_k(k) &= \mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) - \mathbf{K}(k) \mathbf{M}(k) \mathbf{K}'(k) \\ \mathbf{XÌ‚}_k(k) &= \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k) & \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k) & \cdots & \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k) \end{matrix}\bigg] + \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{0} & \gamma \sqrt{\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k}(k)} & - \gamma \sqrt{\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k}(k)} \end{matrix}\bigg] \\ \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \bigg[\begin{matrix} \mathbf{fÌ‚}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}^{1}(k), \mathbf{u}(k), \mathbf{d}(k) \Big) & \mathbf{fÌ‚}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}^{2}(k), \mathbf{u}(k), \mathbf{d}(k) \Big) & \cdots & \mathbf{fÌ‚}\Big( \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}^{n_Ïƒ}(k), \mathbf{u}(k), \mathbf{d}(k) \Big) \end{matrix}\bigg] \\ \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}(k+1)\mathbf{mÌ‚} \\ \mathbf{XÌ„}_k(k+1) &= \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{XÌ‚}_{k}^{1}(k+1) - update_estimate!(estim::ExtendedKalmanFilter, u, ym, d=Float64[])

Update ExtendedKalmanFilter state estim.xÌ‚ and covariance estim.PÌ‚.

The equations are similar to update_estimate!(::KalmanFilter) but with the substitutions $\mathbf{AÌ‚ = FÌ‚}(k)$ and $\mathbf{CÌ‚^m = HÌ‚^m}(k)$:

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{M}(k) &= \mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)\mathbf{HÌ‚^m}' [\mathbf{HÌ‚^m PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)\mathbf{HÌ‚^m + RÌ‚}]^{-1} \\ \mathbf{K}(k) &= \mathbf{FÌ‚ M(k)} \\ \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k) &= \mathbf{hÌ‚^m}\Big( \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k), \mathbf{d}(k) \Big) \\ \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \mathbf{fÌ‚}\Big( \mathbf{xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k), \mathbf{u}(k), \mathbf{d}(k) \Big) + \mathbf{K}(k)[\mathbf{y^m}(k) - \mathbf{yÌ‚^m}(k)] \\ \mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k}(k+1) &= \mathbf{FÌ‚}[\mathbf{PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k) - \mathbf{M}(k)\mathbf{HÌ‚^m PÌ‚}_{k-1}(k)]\mathbf{FÌ‚}' + \mathbf{QÌ‚} \end{aligned}\]

ForwardDiff.jacobian automatically computes the Jacobians:

\[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{FÌ‚}(k) &= \left. \frac{âˆ‚\mathbf{fÌ‚}(\mathbf{xÌ‚}, \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{d})}{âˆ‚\mathbf{xÌ‚}} \right|_{\mathbf{xÌ‚ = xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k),\, \mathbf{u = u}(k),\, \mathbf{d = d}(k)} \\ \mathbf{HÌ‚}(k) &= \left. \frac{âˆ‚\mathbf{hÌ‚}(\mathbf{xÌ‚}, \mathbf{d})}{âˆ‚\mathbf{xÌ‚}} \right|_{\mathbf{x = xÌ‚}_{k-1}(k),\, \mathbf{d = d}(k)} \end{aligned}\]

The matrix $\mathbf{HÌ‚^m}$ is the rows of $\mathbf{HÌ‚}$ that are measured outputs.
